# Sean Barraclough — Writing Guidelines

These rules apply to all copy written for or about Sean Barraclough — CV, portfolio, bio, project descriptions, or any personal writing.

---

## Voice and tone

Write like a capable, thoughtful professional who does not need to oversell himself. Direct and confident without being boastful. The work should speak — the writing just needs to present it clearly.

Calm but not dry. Precise but not vague. If something is worth saying, say it plainly.

---

## Sentence structure

- Short sentences for key facts. Longer ones for context and explanation.
- Vary the rhythm. Back-to-back short sentences feel robotic. Back-to-back long ones lose the reader.
- Use active voice as the default. "He built the system" not "The system was built by him."
- Never start consecutive paragraphs with the same word.

---

## Specific rules

- Do not open with "In [year]..." or "Every [time period]..."
- Do not end sections with a rhetorical question.
- Do not use em dashes. Use a regular dash with a space either side, or rewrite the sentence.
- Do not pad. If something can be said in one sentence, say it in one sentence.
- Do not moralise or philosophise unprompted.
- British English spelling throughout: metre not meter, colour not color, centre not center.

---

## Banned words and phrases

Never use any of the following under any circumstances.

**AI clichés:**
delve, underscore, pivotal, realm, harness, illuminate, revolutionise, innovative, cutting-edge, game-changing, transformative, seamless, bolster, facilitate, shed light on, streamline, that being said, at its core, it's worth noting, in the ever-evolving landscape, from a broader perspective, a key takeaway, generally speaking, broadly speaking, it is important to note, in conclusion, arguably, to some extent

**CV/portfolio clichés:**
passionate, driven, results-oriented, self-starter, proactive, detail-oriented, thought leader, team player, problem-solver, go-getter, dynamic, proven track record, wear many hats, hit the ground running, move the needle

**Vague qualifiers:**
very, quite, rather, fairly, somewhat, incredibly, extremely (use only when a more precise word genuinely does not exist)

---

## The reader

Write for someone who wants to know quickly whether Sean is the right person for a role or project. They are time-pressed and have seen a hundred CVs. Give them the clearest possible picture of what he does and what he has built — without filler, without hype.
